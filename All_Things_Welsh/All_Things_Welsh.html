Introduction

Wales is a country with distinctive traditions, heritage and culture.

It is known as a land of song and a country steeped in myth and legend, which, when you see the awe-inspiring landscape, is no surprise.

Key Facts and Figures

  • Capital: Cardiff
  • Population: 3.1m
  • Size: 20,800 square km
  • Currency: Pound Sterling
  • National Day: St David's Day, 1st March
  • National Symbols: Dragon, Daffodil, Leek
  • Language: Welsh, English
  • Cities: Cardiff (capital), Newport, Swansea, Bangor, St Davids, St Asaph and Wrexham
Welsh Feathers

The feathers are the badge of the heir apparent to the British throne regardless of whether or not the Prince of Wales title is held. It is commonly used to symbolise Wales, particulary in the WRU (Welsh Rugby Union)

Love Spoons

The art of carving Welsh love spoons dates back to the 17th-century, where men would spend hours decoratively carving the handle of a wooden spoon, before presenting it to the object of their affections as a token of love and affection.

Pen-Y-Fan

Pen-Y-Fan is the highest peak in South Wales, situated in Brecon Beacons National Park. Elevation: 886m (2,907ft) Prominence: 672m (2,205ft)
